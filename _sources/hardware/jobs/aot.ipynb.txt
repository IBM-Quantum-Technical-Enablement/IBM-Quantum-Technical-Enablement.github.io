    "\n",
    "When submitting a job to an IBM Quantum system, there is a lot that goes on behind the scenes.  Some of this is visible if, for example, you go look at your jobs on the IBM Quantum website (https://quantum-computing.ibm.com/)."

    "Here we are concerned with what is happening during the \"Running\" stage of the process, where the target quantum system has pulled the job from the queue, and is starting its execution.\n",
    "\n",
    "Circuit execution can be thought of as consisting of three pieces. 1) A pre-processing step, where an input circuit (represented in a serializable format such as QASM3) is converted into an intermediate representation, and the gates / instructions in the circuit converted into waveforms.  2)  The waveforms are triggered to perfrom the actual circuit executions. 3) Finally there is a post-processing step that takes all of the samples collected via qubit measurements and returns them as a result.  If jobs are executed in serial, this can lead to non-negligible amounts of idle time on the processor:"
   ]

    "As circuit compilation and waveform generation for one job is independent from others, it is possible to perform these operations ahead of time if the output can be cached until the circuits are ready to be executed."
   ]

    "Starting at the beginning of 2023, IBM Quantum systems began doing this compilation and waveform generation simultaneously for a collection of jobs ahead of their actual execution time.  This \"ahead of time compilation\" (AoT) removes the overhead from this pre-processing step, yielding additional throughput on the machines."
